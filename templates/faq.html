How accurate is the heart disease prediction system?

Which factors are used to predict heart disease?

Factors include age, gender, blood pressure, cholesterol level, blood sugar, ECG results, heart rate, and lifestyle indicators.

Can this system detect heart disease at an early stage?

The system can indicate early risk patterns but cannot replace clinical tests or professional diagnosis.

Is heart disease prediction safe to use?

Yes. The system is safe for educational and awareness purposes, but it should not be used for medical treatment decisions.

Can this prediction replace medical tests?

No. It cannot replace ECG, blood tests, or doctor consultations. It only assists in understanding potential risk.

What machine learning algorithms are used?

Algorithms such as Logistic Regression, Support Vector Machine, Random Forest, and XGBoost are commonly used for heart disease prediction.

How does the model make predictions?

The model analyzes patterns in historical patient data and compares them with user input to estimate heart disease risk.

Can the prediction results be wrong?

Yes. Incorrect or incomplete input data and model limitations may affect prediction accuracy.

Is user data stored or shared?

No. User data is processed temporarily and is not stored or shared with third parties.

Who should use heart disease prediction systems?

Students, healthcare researchers, and individuals who want to understand heart health risks can use this system.

Can lifestyle changes reduce predicted heart disease risk?

Yes. Healthy diet, regular exercise, stress management, and avoiding smoking can help reduce heart disease risk.
